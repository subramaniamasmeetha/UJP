Editorial Board Roles & Responsibilities

Join our team for Issue XI! We are recruiting undergraduate and graduate students to assist in the publication process. Review the roles below and email sfuujp@sfu.ca with your CV and transcript to apply.

Undergraduate Reviewers

Duties occur in June-July

  • Evaluate submissions based on established criteria (originality, ethics, structure).
  • Provide constructive feedback to authors.
  • Meet with graduate reviewer to discuss process and create a plan (30-45 minutes).
  • Read and evaluate submission independently (~1 hour).
  • Discuss reviews with graduate partner prior to posting feedback (1-1.5 hours).
  • Write first draft of letter to author and collaborate on final letter (~2 hours).
Graduate Reviewers

Duties occur in June-July

  • Conduct rigorous reviews of submissions in areas of expertise.
  • Mentor undergraduate reviewers on assigned papers.
  • Meet with undergraduate partner to discuss the review process (~1 hour).
  • Conduct independent review (~1-2 hours) and discuss with partner (1-1.5 hours).
  • Review and provide feedback to undergraduate's letter to the author.
Editor and Copyeditors

Duties occur in August-Sept

  • Edit and proofread accepted manuscripts for grammar, punctuation, style, and clarity.
  • Ensure adherence to APA formatting and journal guidelines.
  • Collaborate with authors to address editorial suggestions.
  • Maintain consistency and professionalism in the journal's writing style.
  • Approximately 1-1.5 hours of work per assigned paper.
Administrative & Publishing Assistants

Duties occur February-September

  • Admin Assistant: Increase online presence, act as representative at events, support submission process, and liaise with authors/reviewers.
  • Publishing Assistant: Handle logistics of publication, track deadlines, and engage with the audience post-publication.
Managing Editors & Advisors
  • Managing Editors: Oversee operations, make final decisions on peer-review, manage budgeting, and collaborate to ensure timely publication.
  • Graduate Advisor: Provide mentorship to graduate reviewers and facilitate communication between graduate/undergraduate teams.
  • Faculty Advisor: Offer academic expertise, ensure alignment with academic standards, and assist in conflict resolution.
